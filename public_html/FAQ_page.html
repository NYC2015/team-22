FAQ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is KCA?

A: Keep a Child Alive (KCA) is a nonprofit organization whose mission is to realize the end of AIDS for children and families by combating the physical, social, and economic impacts of HIV. The organization was founded in 2003, by Alicia Keys and Leigh Blake, as an emergency push to get life-saving HIV medications to African children and families, who otherwise would not have access to treatment.

Q: What are the advantages of using this website?

A: Users may:
- share experiences and provide support to each other about the realities of living with HIV and how to live positively and confidently.
- find basic, evidence-based information on HIV and sexual and reproductive health.
- get a greater understanding of the resources and services available to young people locally for HIV care and psychosocial support.
- reach out for assistance/advice from experts or youth peer educators ("Cyber Buddies").
- receive HIV clinical care and appointment reminders and daily medication reminders.

Q: If I use this website, will my personal information be at risk?

A: Users have the choice to remain anonymous or not. This setting can be changed at any time by the user.

Q: I want to help children with AIDs. What can I do?

A: People who want to do more to help the children with AIDs in their communities may act as youth peer educators ("Cyber Buddies") or moderators for the forums. If you interested, click on the Join Us tab in the selection menu for more information.
